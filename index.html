• 1876: George Carey from America first described a full recording and reproducing camera system based on selenium.

  • 1880: Alexander Graham Bell and Thomas Edison started working on a telephone system that could transmit an image.

  • 1884: German technician and inventor Paul Nipkow transmitted a static image with 18 horizontal lines of resolution.

  • 1924: Scottish inventor John Baird invented the first TV made of cardboard and a bicycle lamp, which was sold commercially five years later.

  • 1947: Two milestone television programs debuted: Howdy Doody and Meet the Press.
